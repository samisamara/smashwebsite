How To Play

Super Samsh Bros Ultimate has plenty of ways to attack, defend, and move about the stage! It can be a bit much to process all at once since Super Smash Bros is very different from your usual fighting game, however, these tips and tricks should help you ge the basics down!

Jump Jumping is a key part of Super Smash Bros. To jump, simply press the x or y buttons, and to double jump, do the same but in mid air. You can also jump by tilting the anolog stick upward if you have the option turned on. All characters have different jumping heights, and some characters can jump more than twice, such as Kirby, Meta Knight, and more.
Running and Walking Running is just as important to smash as jumping is. To walk, move the anolog stick left or right. To run, move the anolog stick left or right very quickly. Sonic is the fastest runner in the entire game.
Sheild Sheilding is very important and is a deciding factor on if you win or lose. To sheild, press ZL or ZR. This will allow you to block attacks and projectiles. Don't use it too much or hold it too long or else your sheild will break. Certain moves can completely break them if fully charged. If your sheild is broken, you'll be stunned for a while and be left open for attacks.
Grab and Throws Grabbing is equally as important as sheilding. To grab an opponent, press L or R when the opponent is close. If the grab is successful, you can press L or R repeatedly to pummel the opponent. Don't do this for too long or else they'll escape! After a grab or a pummel, you can tilt the anolog stick in one of 4 directions to throw them!
Neutral A These attacks are basic jabs, punches and kicks. They differ in range, type, and power for each character, however that can be said for all attacks. To perform a neutral A attack, simply press A. Most characters can perform extra hits by press A over and over to perform a jab or three hit combo.
Tilts Tilts are a great way of stringing combos at low percentages. They all are performed by pressing A while holding a direction.
Dash Attack Dash attacks are the attacks mainly used to apply pressure on the opponent. In many cases, you can use dash attacks to begin a combo. To perform one, simply press A while you're already running.
Smash Attacks Smash attacks are your main method of getting KOs. Press A and a direction at the same time to perform a smash attack. If you press A and a direction but hold for a bit, you'll perform a stronger smash attack!
Arials Arial attacks are best for situations that involve being in mid air such as a falling opponent or recovering to the stage. If you press A while holding up, down, forward or backward, you can perform different arial attacks. There is also an arial command for pressing A without a direction. You can also use the right analog stick to perfrom arials if you don't want to move around while using an arial attack.
B moves (Specials) B moves are your game changers and ultimately what makes each character so unique. Mario can throw fire balls, Sonic can use the spindash, Kirby can inhale opponents and copy THEIR B moves, and so on. Just like arial attacks, you can pressing B while holding a direction, or press B without pressing a direction.
Up Special (Up B) Although this is part of the special moves section, Up special is especially important. This is essencially your "third jump". It is also an attack for most characters. It is mostly refered to as a recovery, because it's meant to help you recover to the stage after getting knocked off.
Dodge Dodging can be performed by pressing a direction and the sheild button at the same time. This allows you to completely avoid attacks. If you use it too much in a short period of time, it will become slower and you'll get hit more easily. You can also dodge in mid air!
Ledge Grab If you are off the stage and get close enough to the ledge, you will grab it automatically. After you've grabbed the ledge, you can either roll back to the stage, perform a ledge attack, do a ledge jump, or simply just get back up.
Taunts For the most part, taunts don't actually do any damage. They are mostly there to perform a mini-celebration after getting a KO, and to give each fighter a bit of personality! Most taunts are a refrence to something from the character's original series! To taunt, press up, down, or sideways on the D-pad.
